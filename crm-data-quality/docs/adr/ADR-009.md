# ADR-009: Score Persistence and Write-Back Strategy

## Status
Accepted

## Context
Quality scores must be consumable by downstream CRM processes while keeping integration internals decoupled. The persistence model influences traceability and remediation workflows.

## Decision
Persist quality scores and rule outcomes to dedicated extension tables/columns designed for integration ownership, then expose curated projections for CRM workflows.

## Consequences
- Positive: Clear ownership and audit trail for scoring outputs.
- Positive: Limits collision risk with core business field semantics.
- Negative: Requires schema extension and migration discipline.
- Negative: Additional mapping for workflow consumers.

## Alternatives Considered
- Overwrite existing business fields directly: rejected due to semantic conflict risk.
- Keep scores external only: rejected because native CRM remediation workflows need local availability.
